+ The Long

I always find it a little difficult in introducing myself because.. what do I say first?

I'm a photographer, a front end developer, a designer, and a goal-oriented student with insatiable and eclectic curiosity. Based in San Diego, I'm a Bay Area native with an atrophied adaptibility to colder temperatures. You can find projecting (or mostly falling off of) new routes at the rock climbing gym. You can find me up at 2am after a long work day learning how to implement the newest trends in front end development. You can find me frequenting my favorite bakery for freshly baked crossaints. You can find me behind the camera and impatiently waiting for photos to upload to my creative cloud account on Lightroom.

To me, the experience as a photographer encompasses traveling to cool/new places, saving important snapshots in time, meeting other creatives, learning to better understand human emotion/behavior, and ultimately anticipating and captuing the perfect shot. I am currently working on, what will be, my first self-published landscape photography book.

A few years ago, I completed my B.S. in Bioengineering (Electrical Engineering track) from the University of California, San Diego, where I published my first research publication in cardiovascular image processing, raced competitively in collegiate road cycling, and drank too many cups of coffee to count. For 40 hours/week as a Software Engineer at Illumina, I drive the development of instrument software workflows used to operate, generate, and analyze bioinformatics data from the sequencers.
